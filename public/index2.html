About Me

After graduating with a BA in Japanese Language and Literature from the University of Georgia, I enlisted in the U.S. Army for four years. During this time I served as a target audience analyst and cultural expert over the course of two deployments to Afghanistan while simultanesouly earning my MA in International Relations.

Upon leaving the Army, I spent two months on a solo bike tour across the U.S. before moving to Harbin, China for 7 months to study Chinese. After moving to Japan for three years to attend the Communication Studies program at Temple University in Tokyo, Japan, I returned home to join Wyncode Academy's 9-week coding bootcamp in Fort Lauderdale, FL. At Wyncode we study a wide range of topics including Ruby on Rails, JavaScript, HTML5/CSS5 and Bootstrap.

When I'm not immersed in coding, I enjoy photography, traveling and learning new languages.

EnAble Japan

I manage EnAble Japan, which is a site dedicated to helping foreigners get the most out of their stay in Tokyo.

In addition to completing the site redesign, I have updated the web copy and improved the SEO. Additionally, I manage the social media accounts, edit photographs and video, and serve as editor.

Dig Travel Magazine

In late 2013, I co-founded DIG Travel Magazine, an online magazine geared towards empowering women to go out and see the world. As Editor-in-Chief, I work with guest bloggers, manage our Twitter account and write articles. For samples of my articles, please visit my author page.

Betterish JLPT

I founded Betterish JLPT because as I was preparing for the N2 level of the JLPT (Japanese Language Proficiency Test) in 2013, I realized that Japanese self-study resources were painfully inadequate. Though I passed, I wanted to help others learn from my (many) mistakes.

At Betterish, I work as the editor, main writer and am responsible for the site maintenance and design.

WynOverflow

One difficulty in attending a coding bootcamp is that you need as much time with TAs as possible. So for our first group assignment at Wyncode, our team created WynOverflow. This forum gives students a place to both ask questions about homework assignments and to learn from other students' solutions. This way TAs can spend less time repeating themselves and students improve their code reading skills.
