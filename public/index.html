About Me

My name is Krystal Klumpp and I'm a web developer at Vitacost. I work with JavaScript, Ruby on Rails, HTML5, CSS3 & responsive design techniques.

After graduating with a BA in Japanese Language and Literature from the University of Georgia, I took the next logical step and enlisted in the U.S. Army. While in the Army I worked as a target audience analyst and cultural expert over the course of two deployments to Afghanistan. which is an absolutely fascinating country.

Post-Army I spent two months on a solo bike tour across the U.S. before moving to Harbin, China for 7 months. After Harbin taught me what cold really means, I headed over to Japan where I lived off of ramen and Pocky for 3 years (yes, I know you're weeping with jealousy right now).

In Tokyo, I attended the Communication Studies program at Temple University in Tokyo, Japan. Though I had intended to become a journalist, I quickly became more interested in how information was being presented online rather than in the information itself. So I returned home to join Wyncode Academy's 9-week Ruby on Rails-focused coding bootcamp in Fort Lauderdale, FL. In addition to my work at Vitacost, I'm a TA at Wyncode. I openly admit that I share my love of flexbox and JavaScript with the students in the hopes of corrupting them and converting them into front end developers. Or at the very least full stack developers.

When I'm not immersed in coding, I enjoy photography, traveling and learning new languages.
